📚 Quick Review Notes

Click any thumbnail to expand.

⭐ 1. Pelvic Bones (Pg 91)

  • 4 Bones: 2 Innominate, 1 Sacrum, 1 Coccyx.
  • Innominate = Ilium + Ischium + Pubis (fused).

⭐ 2. Inlet/Cavity/Outlet (Pg 93)

  • Definitions of the AP, Transverse, and Oblique diameters.
  • Cavity is circular (all 12cm).

⭐ 3. Diameters Table (Pg 93)

  • Brim: AP 11 | Oblq 12 | Trans 13
  • Outlet: AP 13 | Oblq 12 | Trans 11

⭐ 4. Pelvic Types (Pg 95)

  • 4 Types: Gynecoid, Android, Anthropoid, Platypelloid.
  • Contracted: reduced by 1cm+ (Leads to CPD).

⭐ 5. Gynecoid Pelvis (Pg 96)

  • 50% normal female. Rounded brim.
  • Ideal for childbearing (90° arch).

⭐ 6. Luteal Phase (Pg 124)

  • Corpus luteum -> Progesterone (2 wks).
  • Prepares endometrium for fertilized oocyte.

⭐ 7. Menstrual Phase (Pg 123)

  • Estrogen & Progesterone fall if no fertilization.
  • Simultaneous with beginning of follicular phase.

⭐ 8. Menstrual Hormones (Pg 124)

  • Prostaglandin release -> Spiral artery spasm.
  • Endometrium dies (Necrosis) and sheds to basal layer.

⭐ 9. Bleeding Terms (Pg 124)

  • Eumenorrhoea: Normal (50-150mL, 3-5 days).
  • Menorrhagia: Heavy bleeding. Dysmenorrhoea: Cramps.

⭐ 10. Presumptive Signs (Slide 10)

  • Amenorrhea (4w) | Nausea (4-14w)
  • Quickening (16-20w) | Pigmentation (16w)
